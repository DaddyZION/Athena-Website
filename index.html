Introduction

The education sector is made up of many flaws and struggles, from students' struggling to be engaged and feeling unprepared for their futures, to teachers drowning in preparation work for their lessons and struggling to balance work and rest. This isn't a collection of separate issues but an interconnected, systemic crisis. Research quantifies the problem starkly: 44% of K-12 teachers report feeling burned out "very often" or "always", making it the most burned-out profession in the US [3]. Simultaneously, a "relevance gap" has opened between education and the workforce, with nearly half of recent graduates (48%) feeling unprepared to even apply for entry-level jobs in their field [4]. Our system will aim to aid schools in relieving these struggles, by generating exciting work based on students' unique interests, and freeing up teachers' time.

Athena is an application which allows teachers and students to curate personalised custom group coursework projects, mock exams or tutorials. For example, if a teacher wants to show their class how to integrate a function and apply it to real world scenarios, usually the whole class would learn from the same examples. Using Athena, the teacher can specify using natural language the topic, how many different examples, as well as any specification they require, and using sophisticated prompt engineering and state of the art AI, Athena can create the lessons. Athena is used by both student and teacher allowing for easy integration into the classroom, including a learning coach to go through and help the students when they are struggling. This frees the teachers time to focus on helping the students where they individually get stuck and allows the students to collaborate on a more diverse set of applications for the given subject.

Engagement Deficit

"One in four pupils in England 'disengage' when they move up to secondary school." [1]

This crisis of engagement is driven by standardized curricula that fail to connect with students. This creates a significant perception gap: while 63% of students report feeling highly engaged, only 45% of teachers agree, suggesting the reality is far more concerning than self-reports indicate [5]. Educational research identifies four key dimensions of engagement often missing in traditional models: relevance, autonomy, collaboration, and authenticity [6]. When students see a task as relevant to their interests, have control over their learning path, can work with peers, and tackle real-world problems, their motivation and performance increase significantly [6].

A systemic issue deepens this problem. While 72% of teachers see "asking thoughtful questions" as the best sign of engagement, 54% of superintendents identify "performing well on assessments" as the top indicator [5]. This conflict forces teaching methods that are optimized for standardized tests, even when those methods are known to undermine the active, authentic learning that fosters deep understanding.

How would Athena help?

  • Athena will personalise the learning experience and this would assist students by personalising examples in ways which they can easily understand based on perhaps the already level of understanding and meeting the learning objectives. This directly fosters relevance and autonomy, two of the core drivers of engagement.
  • By doing the point above, it would help with taking a "burden" off the teachers, as it is automatically done, all they would have to do at the early stage would be to briefly check that the example given is suitable.

Possible Shortcomings:

  • Athena may not be as engaging to students who have mitigating circumstances, no matter how much the work is structured towards their interests, if they have things that are distracting in their life, they may still not be engaged.
  • "Pupils eligible for free school meals report lower levels of trust, enjoyment, and belonging; the gap widens as secondary school progresses." [1]

Student & Teacher Burnout & Inefficiencies

"The main causes of heavy workload are:

  • the volume of administrative tasks
  • the volume of marking
  • staff shortages
  • lack of support from external specialist agencies such as for special educational needs and disabilities (SEND), or behaviour
  • challenging behaviour of pupils
  • changes to external examinations
  • frequently changing government policies and regulations
  • in some cases, lack of skills or training" [2]

The scale of the burnout crisis is alarming. A National Education Association (NEA) survey found that 90% of members described burnout as a "very serious" or "somewhat serious" issue [3]. This has led to a mass exodus, with over 300,000 public school teachers and staff leaving their jobs between February 2020 and May 2022. A 2022 poll revealed 55% of current teachers plan to leave the profession sooner than intended [3].

A key driver of this overwhelming workload is the demand for differentiation‚Äîthe process of manually tailoring instruction for dozens of students with diverse learning needs [7]. This impossible burden creates a vicious cycle: disengaged students require more differentiation, which increases teacher workload and causes burnout. That burnout then reduces teaching quality, which further exacerbates student disengagement [8].

How would Athena help?

  • Athena would help teachers combat the heavy workload they have to deal with as a consequence of becoming a teacher. By automating the generation of personalized, project-based curricula, it directly tackles the unsustainable burden of manual differentiation.
  • As a consequence of combating their heavy workload this will increase teacher satisfaction which creates external benefits for society this also means that teachers job retention will be higher as they would enjoy working in a school environment that's not dominated by admin related work - especially for the staff in school that are supposed to teach the students.
  • This will mean that issues like staff shortages will also be diminished, as teachers will be happy with their role at school and it also makes staff shortage less of a problem as majority of the marking will not be done by teachers but by Athena. However, proof reading the marking will be required.
  • Athena would help reduce the high volume of marking required from teachers. By automating assessment marking and tasking, it allows teachers to dedicate more time to activities such as developing improved teaching materials and providing personalised support to students, especially those who have mitigating circumstances.

Possible Shortcomings:

  • The success of students is not fully determined by teachers. "Poor behaviour in the classroom ( low-level disruption, absenteeism, verbal abuse )" - stems from factors outside school, it includes the students home and socioeconomic status. This demonstrates that student achievement is influenced by a range of factors beyond the teachers control. [2]
  • Considering that this will be a new addition in a school, it will mean that by trying to get rid of 'admin work' for teachers, it actually creates overall more admin work for the other staff, which can be argued is worse off, as the other staff in a school that manage will have a potentially worse wellbeing due to the high work load now they have to deal with.
  • There are other causes of stress for both students and teachers [2]

  • Centralisation of data: Compiles all data into a single repository, allowing a comprehensive view of student resources, progress and administrative operations. This reduces redundancy in data through streamlined operations.
  • Improves communication: This system promotes connectivity by creating a shared space where users are allowed to communicate through messages, comments, or by sharing feedback.
  • Provides data-driven insights: The system allows users to manage their own learning plans, Consequently, the compilation of data on student activity provides real-time information to teachers, personal tutors and parents to monitor student performance.
  • Bridging the Relevance Gap: By grounding learning in authentic, student-chosen projects, Athena directly addresses the gap between academic skills and workforce demands. It helps solve the "paradox of preparation," where students learn timeless skills like critical thinking but can't demonstrate them in the practical, technical contexts employers use for screening. Students graduate not just with knowledge, but with a portfolio of tangible work that proves their competency.

Categories of Users

The system is aimed at primary and secondary schools, with the purpose of making learning more engaging and exciting for students by catering to their individual interests, as well as easing the workload of teachers by quickly generating lesson plans and coursework ideas. The five main user categories are teachers, students, parents/guardians, administrators, and personal tutors.

Figure: Communication pathways between different user categories in the Athena system

1. Teachers

A teacher using the system would have the ability to define core learning objectives, in order to generate learning materials, in response to personal interests proposed by the students. They also have the ability to create groups and manage groups consisting of different students, in order to assign them tasks and monitor their submissions.

  • User Stories:
    • As a teacher, I want to quickly create group work for the class so that students are kept engaged during their learning, at the start of each term, in order to make my students interested and save me time in my busy schedule.
    • As a teacher, I need to view the progress of my students work so I can see the areas they are doing well or struggling in, every evening, because I need to plan the lessons for the next day.
  • System Functionalities:
    • Define core learning objectives
    • Generate materials such as: tests and mock exams, tutorials and worksheets, group projects and coursework
    • Create and manage student groups
    • Assign tasks
    • Monitor students' submissions

2. Students

Students would be able to access both the Classroom features and Personal Revision features. In the classroom feature the student would be able to connect to the group created by teachers (or friends/family) and access the curated projects/exams to work on. The app will feature an intuitive UI with clear instructions how to use it, so the student can easily use it. The student will be able to input what their interests are which the app can save for future reference, so the teacher can tell Athena to create a specific learning outcome exam/project which is tailored to the students interests.

  • User stories:
    • As a student I want to be able to engage with my learning in a way that excites me and is applicable to my interests, since I need to be able to understand how the subject I'm learning can be applied in the real world by learning it through a hands-on approach.
    • As a student who is struggling to understand the content, I need to access personalised revision features which clearly explain the content, so that I can better understand the subject and learn faster.
    • As a student finds the content easy, I want to find extension work catered to my interests so that I can continue my learning independently when I have completed the work set by my teacher, because otherwise I feel bored.
  • System Functionalities:
    • Access Classroom and Personal Revision features
    • Connect and communicate with a group
    • Work on and submit learning materials set by teachers
    • Input personal interests to be saved for future use
    • Receive learning tasks tailored to their interests

3. Parents/Guardians

Guardians are users which will need access to grades and be able to see the types of examples/questions the students are given, and offer support where needed. There is also a clear line of communication on Athena that allows the parents to arise any issues with the Admins/Teachers.

  • User stories:
    • As a parent/guardian I want to be able to see how Athena is impacting my children's grades, and perhaps even see their interests, so that I could help them get a better understanding or inform their tutors about this interests. Showing interest to my child's work and being able to understand it, monitor their hobbies/workloads, is important for their development.
    • As a parent/guardian I expect to know how my children are performing, so that I can make sure that they are on track with their studies and that they are understanding the work they are doing at school, as well as being able to monitor their grades and address any issues regarding their studies.
  • System Functionalities:
    • Access their child's grades, and monitor their improvement
    • See examples of the work their child is assigned
    • View the interests entered by their child
    • Communicate with teachers, personal tutors, and admin

4. Admin

An administrator's role is to have oversight of the system and ensure students are performing well, by being able to access academic data of the whole school.

  • User Stories:
    • As an admin I need to be able to access all student's academic data so that I am able to ensure the students are performing well throughout the course because if there are any issues that arise, I need to be able to contact their teachers and guardians to correct the issue, as soon as possible.
    • As an admin, I want to review if teachers are fully utilising Athena so that teaching quality can be to the best standard. This would be done regularly.
  • System Functionalities:
    • Access the grades and other academic data of every student in the school
    • View an overview of academic data, categorised by year groups, subjects, or teachers
    • Review teachers' usages of the system
    • Contact specific teachers or parents in order to correct issues

5. Personal Tutor

A tutor's role is to provide teaching assistance and tailored teaching resources to students. Tutors focus on identifying students' strengths and weaknesses to create a supportive learning environment through constructive feedback while addressing students' specific needs.

  • User Stories:
    • As a tutor, I need a summary of my tutee's academic performances because I want to identify their areas of growth and improvement so that I can cater my learning plans accordingly.
    • As a tutor, I want to provide sufficient feedback on students' assignments so that I can personally cater to each students' needs while working with a large number of tutees.
  • System Functionalities:
    • Assign exercises and monitor tutees' progress
    • Create personalised learning materials consistent to tutees' needs
    • Analyse and generate summarised reports on tutee performance
    • Provide feedback on tutees' submissions

Data Analysis

Based on the features available in the system, the app will need to store the following knowledge, for each user category.

1. Teachers

Information used:

  • API - This is needed to generate questions and coursework with accurate knowledge about different subjects, ensuring all teaching and questions are providing students with correct information.
  • Information about individual teachers - This is entered into a database by administrators upon enrolling in the system. Information will include name, position, and subjects and year groups taught.
  • Classes assigned to - This is entered into a database by administrators at the start of each academic year, to keep track of which students the teacher teaches, allowing work to be assigned to whole classes or smaller groups of students.

2. Students

Information used:

  • Personal interests - This will be entered by the students, and stored for future reference, in order to generate engaging questions and projects about their individual interests.
  • Data about individual students - This is entered into a database by administrators upon enrolling in the system. Information will include name, gender, age, and which subjects and classes they are in. This is used to provide students with work individually catered to them.

3. Parents/Guardians

Information used:

  • Contact information such as email addresses - This will be entered by the user and used to keep parents up to date with their child's progress.
  • Name of and data relating to their child - Parents/guardians can only see information about their own child.

4. Admin

Information used:

  • Data about individual students - This is entered into a database by administrators upon enrolling in the system. Information will include name, gender, age, and which subjects and classes they are in. This is used to analyse data about students and track the progress of different year groups, subjects, or classes, to ensure they are making the most of the system.
  • Information about individual teachers - This is entered into a database by administrators upon enrolling in the system. Information will include name, position, and subjects and year groups taught.

5. Personal Tutors

Information used:

  • API - This is used to modify learning plans and generate educational resources, featuring accurate knowledge about different subject areas.
  • Information on individual tutees - This is used to analyse data on student performance, and cater to individual student needs.

Based on this framework, Athena will need access to and/or store the following information:

  • User Profiles
  • Courses (Titles, subject, level/grade, assigned instructor)
  • Interaction history (AI chats, performance, feedbacks)
  • Educational Resources: AI generated lessons, Book references
  • Performance: Quiz scores, Weekly/Monthly statistics on attendance/progress, Course completion rates
